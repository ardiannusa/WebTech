House DJ Set

House is a genre of Electronic Music that is characterized with a repetitive four-on-the-floor beat and a typical tempo of 120 to 130 BPM. The genre was created by DJs and producers from the Chicago underground scene in the 1980s, as DJs in the sub-culture began to alter disco songs to add deeper basslines and more mechanical beat.

The genre was pioneered by DJs from Chicago and New York City such as Frankie Knuckles, Larry Levan, Ron Hardy, Jesse Saunders, Chip E, Steve Hurley, and Farley Funk, Marshall Jefferson, Phuture, and others. The genre grew internationally to London, other American cities in the Chicago club and is now becoming a worldwide phenomenon. Today, the genre is preserved with popular acts such as DJ Boring and Disclosure, representing two of the best House Sets in the BR platform.

Disclosure is an English electronic music duo that consists of brothers Howard and Guy Lawrence. They grew in Reigate, Surrey. Their debut studio album, "Settle," was released on 3 June 2013 by PMR records, nominated for Best Dance/Electronic Album at the 2014 Grammy Awards. Afterward, on 25 September 2015, they released their second studio album, Caracal, which was also nominated for the Best Dance/Electronic Album at the 2016 Grammy Awards. Their third studio album, Energy, was released on 28 August 2020. They were nominated for Best Dance/Electronic Album at the 2021 Grammy Awards, alongside the fourth single from the album, "My High," which was nominated for Best Dance Recording.
